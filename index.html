My Project

Overview

For my project, I would like to develop a training program for Deli employees at Coles Supermarkets. Context for this project is derived from my current part-time job as a Deli employee at Coles. As a Deli team member, the core duty is to weigh items such as hams, salami, seafood and more and input a code on the scales to print out a ticket which labels the product name, price, weight and use-by date. The program I will develop will take shape in the form of a mini game, where a Deli item will appear on the screen and the user will need to enter the corresponding “PLU” (Price Look-up Code).

Motivation

My motivation for this project stemmed from the recent surge in online orders in the past two years, mostly due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Surges in orders meant more items being required to prepare and pack, while also simultaneously serving face-to-face customers and performing infection control and hygiene duties. As a result, online orders began to increase, so did the number of items required to be prepared. To further compound the issue, new team members were recently hired which meant inexperienced workers and slow packing time.

One correlation is due to the lack of knowledge of PLU (Price Look Up) codes which are displayed at the back of price tickets. Due to the large volume of items on offer, it is understandably difficult to memorise as many as possible, however I find that it affects efficiency and time. In my own trial which I conducted with my co-workers consent, it takes approximately 14 seconds to look-up an item, weigh the item, print the label and wrap the item compared to approximately 6 seconds to repeat the same process without having to look-up the item. This can also be applied to face-to-face service where wait times may be reduced through training.

Description

The main goals for this product is to provide quick and easy knowledge tests which are both informative and informal. Many of the Coles training programs are very formal and basic by design and function, which can make training tasks very unengaging and in many instances, are never completed due to how boring they are and the way employees feel they are “forced” to complete them.

My training program aims to be both informative but also engaging as if employees are playing a mobile game rather than a training module. The program will open with a table which breaks down the Deli department into different categories such as Salami, Cheese, Ham, Smallgoods (Bacons and Frankfurts), Poultry and Seafood. Each category will have a list of every single item being sold with its corresponding PLU code beside it. Once employees begin the program, they will no longer have access to the table and will have to recall as many PLU codes as possible.

Questions will simply be stated as “What is the PLU for Honey Ham?” An image of the product will also be on the screen with a text box underneath to type the corresponding PLU code for the item. If incorrect, it will state that the answer was “Incorrect! The answer is ____.” I’d plan for the screen background to go red for an incorrect answer and green for correct, similar to the Kahoot quiz design. With further studies and practice, I would also like to add sound cues to correspond to the answers whether they are correct or incorrect.

Tools and Technologies

Currently, I am using Python program on the website Trinket. Access to Trinket which is given by the university, has allowed me to use the skills and knowledge taught by my lecturers and in the modules from the Introduction to Programming subject. While the coding is very basic, ideally I would like to expand the code to include multiple choice questions and sound cues for correct and incorrect answers.

Skills Required

As a first year IT student with no experience in the field, I would need to further my skills and understanding in Python programming. In an ideal scenario, I would like to further expand my training program to feature other Deli aspects such as infection control and multiple-choice commands. From my understanding, there are methods of coding which allows repeats of the current code I am currently using without copying and pasting the same code. This is a skill which I have not learned yet but hopefully I can work on it in the future. This is so that employees won’t feel like they’re being forced to complete this program necessary for work, but more like a fun and interactive program which will assist their learning.

Outcome

If this program is successful, ideally online order completions will see a decrease in wait times. With the program’s mini-game nature rather than a training module, hopefully employees may revisit the program multiple times so that it may even be a brain-teaser challenge for them. Additionally, it would also improve wait times for customers who are ordering in-store and work efficiency for the employees who are taking orders.
